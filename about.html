From transistor radios in 1985 to custom gaming rigs in 2026 — Mahesh Electronics has grown with Valsad, one customer at a time.

Our Story

Started in 1985. Still on Halar Road.

Mahesh bhai opened the shop with one Philips transistor, three tape recorders and a repair bench. Valsad needed honest electronics, and Halar Road gave him the foot traffic.

Four decades later, the shop sells gaming PCs, networking gear, Hikvision CCTV and Canon PIXMA printers — but the principle hasn't changed. Every sale is a conversation. Every customer is a name, not a transaction.

Now Rajesh, Mahesh bhai's son, runs the day-to-day. He'll tell you the truth about whether the RTX 4060 is enough for 1440p, or why that cheap CCTV you saw online is a scam. That's why people come back for decades.
